Quadratic equation

When \(a \ne 0\), there are two solutions to \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) and they are $$x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac} \over 2a}.$$

Nottale's equation

$$ d F = \left( \partial_t F + \left( \mathcal{V} \cdot \nabla \right) F - \frac{ i b^2}{2} \nabla^2 F \right) dt + \sqrt{-i} b \left( d \mathcal{Z}_t \cdot \nabla \right) F$$
